Karnataka

1. Bangalore

Bangalore Palace:
- Brief Info: Built in 1887 by the Wodeyar dynasty, showcasing Tudor-style architecture with gothic arches and turrets.
- Architectural Highlights: Ornate stained glass windows, chandeliers, and intricate wood carvings.
- Special Notes: Surrounded by lush gardens, hosts events like exhibitions and concerts, and offers insights into the royal lifestyle of the Wodeyar kings.
- How to Reach: Well connected by road and public transport from Bangalore city center, nearest metro station: Cubbon Park.
- Entry Ticket: INR 230 for adults, INR 460 for foreigners.
View on Google Maps

ISKCON Bangalore:
- Brief Info: Major Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Krishna, known for its grand architecture and serene atmosphere.
- Features: Main temple with deities of Krishna and Radha, auditorium, restaurant, and cultural center.
- Special Notes: Hub for spiritual activities including devotional chanting, yoga classes, and festivals.
- How to Reach: Accessible via public transport, nearest metro station: Mahalakshmi Layout.
- Entry Ticket: No entry fee for the temple; charges apply for special events.
View on Google Maps

Lalbagh Botanical Garden:
- Brief Info: Established in 1760, one of Bangalore’s oldest and largest gardens, covering over 240 acres.
- Features: Glass House, tropical plants, rare species, serene lake, historical structures like Lalbagh Fort and Kempegowda Tower.
- Special Notes: Popular for flower shows, exhibitions, and a peaceful escape from city life.
- How to Reach: Easily reachable via BMTC buses or metro, nearest metro station: Lalbagh.
- Entry Ticket: INR 25 for adults, free for children under 12 years.
View on Google Maps

Bannerghatta National Park:
- Brief Info: Wildlife sanctuary established in 1971, located on the outskirts of Bangalore.
- Features: Diverse habitats including forests, grasslands, and wetlands, home to tigers, lions, elephants, and various birds.
- Special Notes: Offers safari experiences, butterfly park, and zoo.
- How to Reach: About 25 km from Bangalore city center, accessible by BMTC buses and taxis.
- Entry Ticket: INR 80 for adults, safari tickets priced separately.
View on Google Maps

2. Mysore

Mysore Palace:
- Brief Info: Grand royal residence featuring Indo-Saracenic architecture with Hindu, Muslim, Gothic, and Rajput styles.
- Architectural Highlights: Richly decorated rooms, grand Durbar Hall, illuminated façade during Dasara festival.
- Special Notes: Major tourist attraction, reflecting the grandeur of the Wodeyar dynasty.
- How to Reach: Well connected by local buses and auto-rickshaws from Mysore city center.
- Entry Ticket: INR 70 for adults, INR 200 for foreigners.
View on Google Maps

Chamundeshwari Temple:
- Brief Info: Significant Hindu pilgrimage site situated atop Chamundi Hill, dedicated to goddess Chamundeshwari.
- Architectural Highlights: Traditional South Indian style with intricate carvings and towering gopuram.
- Special Notes: Visitors climb 1,000 steps for a rewarding view, vibrant during Chamundi Hill festival.
- How to Reach: About 13 km from Mysore city center, buses and taxis available.
- Entry Ticket: Free entry for general visits, special tickets for fast-track darshan.
View on Google Maps

Somnathpur Temple:
- Brief Info: Renowned for its Hoysala architecture, dedicated to Lord Keshava.
- Architectural Highlights: Intricate carvings and sculptures depicting Hindu mythology, elaborate designs on three main towers.
- Special Notes: UNESCO World Heritage Site, admired for its architectural beauty and historical significance.
- How to Reach: Located 35 km from Mysore, accessible via private taxi or bus.
- Entry Ticket: INR 30 for Indians, INR 500 for foreigners.
View on Google Maps

3. Mangalore

Butterfly Garden:
- Brief Info: Attraction dedicated to butterfly species, featuring various plants and flowers.
- Features: Observing numerous butterfly species, informative displays about their life cycles.
- Special Notes: Educational space promoting conservation and biodiversity.
- How to Reach: Easily accessible by local transport from Mangalore city center.
- Entry Ticket: INR 50 for adults, free for children under 10 years.
View on Google Maps

Panambur Beach:
- Brief Info: Popular beach destination known for its clean sands and serene atmosphere.
- Activities: Camel rides, water sports, food stalls, sunset views.
- Special Notes: Hosts events like kite festivals, attracts both tourists and locals.
- How to Reach: Located 13 km from Mangalore city center, accessible via local buses and taxis.
- Entry Ticket: Free entry.
View on Google Maps

St. Aloysius Chapel:
- Brief Info: Catholic church known for its beautiful frescoes and historical significance.
- Architectural Highlights: Italian-style architecture, stunning wall and ceiling paintings.
- Special Notes: Attracts visitors for both its religious importance and artistic beauty.
- How to Reach: Located in the heart of Mangalore, well connected by local buses and taxis.
- Entry Ticket: No entry fee.
View on Google Maps
