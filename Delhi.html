Delhi

Red Fort:
- Brief Info: A majestic Mughal fortress built by Emperor Shah Jahan in 1648, known for its red sandstone walls and historical significance.
- Architectural Highlights: Diwan-i-Aam, Diwan-i-Khas, and UNESCO World Heritage Site.
- Special Notes: The Prime Minister hoists the national flag here on Independence Day..
View on Google Maps

Akshardham Temple:
- Brief Info: A modern architectural marvel inaugurated in 2005, dedicated to Bhagwan Swaminarayan.
- Architectural Highlights: Intricate carvings, Yagnapurush Kund (largest stepwell), and Sahaj Anand Water Show.
- Special Notes: Includes gardens, a cultural boat ride, and an exhibition hall on Indian history..
View on Google Maps

India Gate:
- Brief Info: A war memorial dedicated to Indian soldiers who died in World War I, designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens and completed in 1931.
- Architectural Highlights: 42 meters tall with inscriptions of over 13,000 soldiers' names.
- Special Notes: Beautifully illuminated at night and a site for national ceremonies..
View on Google Maps

Qutub Minar:
- Brief Info: The tallest brick minaret in the world, standing at 73 meters, built in 1193 by Qutb-ud-din Aibak.
- Architectural Highlights: Intricate carvings, inscriptions in Arabic, part of the Qutub Complex.
- Special Notes: Includes Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque and Iron Pillar of Delhi..
View on Google Maps

Lotus Temple:
- Brief Info: A Bahá'í House of Worship known for its lotus-flower-shaped architecture, completed in 1986.
- Architectural Highlights: 27 marble-clad petals arranged in three tiers.
- Special Notes: Open to people of all religions, surrounded by gardens and reflecting pools..
View on Google Maps

Humayun's Tomb:
- Brief Info: Completed in 1572, it is a precursor to the Taj Mahal and a fine example of Mughal architecture.
- Architectural Highlights: Red sandstone and marble with a central dome and Persian-style gardens.
- Special Notes: UNESCO World Heritage Site, part of a complex with other tombs and monuments..
View on Google Maps

Sarojini Market:
- Brief Info: A bustling shopping destination known for its vibrant atmosphere and diverse range of goods.
- What to Find: Clothing, accessories, home goods, fresh produce.
- Special Notes: Famous for affordable fashion and trendy apparel, lively ambiance..
View on Google Maps

Agrasen Ki Baoli:
- Brief Info: An ancient stepwell in Delhi, believed to be constructed in the 14th century.
- Architectural Highlights: Descending steps, arches, and ornate carvings.
- Special Notes: Originally used for rainwater harvesting and social gatherings, now a popular tourist attraction..
View on Google Maps
