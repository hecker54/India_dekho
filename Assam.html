1. Silchar

Maniharan Temple:
- Brief Info: Ancient Hindu temple associated with Lord Krishna.
- Location: On the banks of the Maniharan stream near Silchar.
- Features: Sacred cave, serene environment.
- Special Notes: Popular during Holi festival, attracting pilgrims and tourists.
- How to Reach: Located 5 km from Silchar city center, accessible by road.
- Entry Ticket: Free entry.

Bhubaneshwar Temple:
- Brief Info: Significant religious site dedicated to Lord Shiva.
- Location: Atop Bhuban Hills near Silchar.
- Features: Challenging trek, breathtaking views.
- Special Notes: Especially crowded during Shivratri festival.
- How to Reach: Accessible by trekking from Silchar, approximately 6 km from the city.
- Entry Ticket: Free entry.
View on Google Maps

Kachari Fort:
- Brief Info: Historical site dating back to the Kachari dynasty.
- Features: Ancient ruins, sculptures, carvings.
- Location: Surrounded by lush greenery in Silchar.
- Special Notes: Peaceful spot for history and archaeology enthusiasts.
- How to Reach: Located 4 km from Silchar city center, accessible by road.
- Entry Ticket: Free entry.
View on Google Maps

Kancha Kanti Devi Temple:
- Brief Info: Temple dedicated to Goddess Kancha Kanti.
- Location: Near Silchar.
- Features: Unique architecture, serene ambiance.
- Special Notes: Busy during Durga Puja and Kali Puja festivals.
- How to Reach: Located 15 km from Silchar, accessible by road.
- Entry Ticket: Free entry.

3. Jorhat

Hoollongapar Gibbon Sanctuary:
- Brief Info: Wildlife sanctuary dedicated to the endangered hoolock gibbon.
- Location: Near Jorhat.
- Features: Dense forests, diverse wildlife.
- Special Notes: Offers birdwatching and nature trails.
- How to Reach: Located 20 km from Jorhat city, accessible by road.
- Entry Ticket: INR 50 per person.
View on Google Maps

Majuli Island:
- Brief Info: Largest river island in the world, rich in culture and heritage.
- Location: In the Brahmaputra River, near Jorhat.
- Features: Satras (monasteries), cultural festivals.
- Special Notes: Known for its vibrant cultural landscape and scenic beauty.
- How to Reach: Accessible via a ferry ride from Jorhat, approximately 20 km.
- Entry Ticket: Free entry.
